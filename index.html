First lets start with a definition. Lets define d as a little bit of.

d “a little bit of”

We can represent an infinitesimaly small change in x with dx. Thus dx means a little bit of x and du means a little bit of u.
Now we can introduce a new symbol ∫ (a squiggly s). This simply means the sum of.

∫ “the sum of”

Thus ∫dx means the sum of all the little bits of x. Or ∫dt means the sum of all the little bits of t. This is called the intergral. If x is considered as made up of a lot of little bits, each of which is called dx, if you add them all up together you get the sum of all the dx's (which is the same thing as the whole of x).

∫ dx "the sum of little bits of x"

The word “integral” simply means “the whole.”
Think of an hour cut up into 3600 little bits called seconds. The whole of the 3600 little bits added up together makes one hour.
